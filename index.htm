Israeli universities complicit in genocide

EPFL is currently collaborating with Israeli universities that are playing a key role in the genocide of the Palestinian people. We will talk about the Hebrew University of Jerusalem (HUJI), Technion (Israel Institute of Technology), Tel Aviv University (TAU), Ben-Gurion University (BGU), the Weizmann Institute, and Bar-Ilan University.

 

Far from the academic principles advocating independence from political and economic power, or from the academic ethics derived from human rights, these universities are fully involved in the development of weapons and military strategies for the Israeli Defense Forces (IDF). These universities are all the more complicit in genocide because none of them has distanced itself from the IDF since its invasion of Gaza. The presidents of all the universities mentioned above have even co-signed a letter calling on the international academic community to align itself with the defense of Israel, which, according to them, is "wrongly labeled an oppressor" [9] . Seventy-five years of destruction and appropriation of Palestinian land are denied by Israeli academics.
Built on stolen land

Let us begin our analysis of the universities with a geographical observation: HUJI and Bar-Ilan University violate international law simply by constructing their buildings. Part of the HUJI campus is built on illegally occupied Palestinian territories in East Jerusalem[10] . Bar-Ilan University built an entire college in an illegal settlement in the West Bank, which has grown into a university in its own right: Ariel University.

 

There are military barracks built on campuses

 

It is difficult to justify academic independence and neutrality when the campus is actually a military training camp. Several universities offer training programs for IDF soldiers. At HUJI, TAU, and BGU, it is normal to encounter those whose daily routine involves shooting children and murdering entire families with missiles. Student soldiers are required to wear military uniforms on campus and sometimes must carry their weapons.

 

Military/university training programs  

HUJI hosts three training programs for IDF officers: Havatzalot, Talpiot, and Tzameret[11] . Classes are taught not by academics but by military personnel; students are housed in barracks reserved for members of the army[12] . The president of HUJI himself encourages all students to come to the university in uniform[13] . Similarly, Tel Aviv University hosts the Erez military program[14] . BGU, for its part, has built a technology campus in conjunction with the IDF.
BGU President Prof. Chamovitz said at the campus opening:

The future growth and development of Ben-Gurion University of the Negev into the North Campus is intimately tied to our growing relationship with the nascent IDF technology and intelligence bases springing up in and around Beer-Sheva. [...] The IDF Technology Campus will include an advanced eco-friendly energy and technology infrastructure. It will bridge the relationship between BGU, the private sector, and the IDF.[15]



University research for military and colonial purposes

Israeli universities are closely linked to the arms industry, with students and researchers participating in weapons design. The examples of projects are too numerous to mention, but they include autonomous demolition bulldozers, drone manufacturing, surveillance systems, and even deafening loudspeakers for crowd control[16] . Elbit Systems has its own research laboratory at Technion[17] , and the university also offers a course on weapons marketing with a focus on promoting the Israeli defense industry internationally[18]

The military involvement of Israeli universities is not anecdotal; it is a structural link. This fact can be directly illustrated by the statements of senior officials in the army and industry:

 

“Technion is part of Elbit's DNA” [19]

-Michael Federmann, president of Elbit Systems, an Israeli

arms manufacturer and the IDF's main supplier of missiles

 

“Military R&D in Israel would not exist without the universities. They carry out all the basic scientific investigation, which is then developed either by defense industries or the army” [20]

-Isaac Ben-Yisrael, who joined TAU after a career as a general and R&D director in the IDF.

Engineering faculties are not the only accomplices in the Israeli regime's colonization enterprise; archaeology is used as a pretext to appropriate and plunder Palestinian territories.  The stratagem is as follows: 1) Classify a site, often inhabited by Palestinians, as archaeological. 2) Evict the inhabitants in order to carry out excavations. 3) Establish a settlement. This scenario has been observed in Susiya [22], Tel Rumeida [23], the City of David [24], Tel Sebastia[25], the Biyar aqueduct [26] , and Tel Shiloh [27]. Israeli universities organize excavations and collaborate with the archaeological authority to classify sites. It is important to note that the archaeological authority is governed by a unit of COGAT [28] [29] , the military administration of the occupied Palestinian territories. Archaeology is not managed by civilians but by the army. Archaeology is not only used as a tool for seizing territory, but also for meticulously erasing Palestinian history. Organized destruction of Palestinian cemeteries[30] ; little or no mention of artifacts and structures belonging to non-biblical periods on the sites; destruction of Islamic excavation layers; Palestine Archaeological Museum renamed "Rockefeller Museum," etc. The examples are numerous. What has happened at EPFL since October 7 ?

 

Since October 12, 2023, EPFL has been unilaterally supporting Israeli victims in a statement that does not mention the word Palestine once, ignoring the incessant bombardments suffered by Palestinians and the colonization carried out by Israel for more than half a century. It was then revealed that EPFL had trained Israeli weapons engineers in the past[33] . The school censored several events on the subject of Palestine[34]  and ignored student petitions calling for change[35] .

On May 1, 2024, after more than 30,000 murders in Gaza and strong pressure from students, the school finally changed its position and mentioned the Palestinian people, but without making any concrete changes to its policy.

 

Pacific occupation launched by the students

In May 2024, students peacefully occupied the SG building twice to demand an end to this policy of censorship and a freeze on collaborations with Israeli universities. Each time, EPFL called the police to expel the students and went so far as to threaten academic and legal action against anyone who wanted to take part in the protests[36]. Several professors and Agepoly condemned this authoritarian drift[37]. Following discussions with the students, the administration committed to taking action on several points: recognizing the Israeli occupation of Palestine, launching an investigation into academic collaborations, and organizing a town hall meeting led by the president. It should be noted here that the EPFL administration, which always advocates dialogue and goodwill, preferred to have peaceful students removed by the police rather than discuss the issue with them. It took civil disobedience by students for the administration to recognize that Israel is illegally occupying Palestinian territories, a colonization that has been known and documented for years by numerous international organizations, including the UN, the International Court of Justice, Human Rights Watch, Amnesty International, etc.

The EPFL therefore categorically refuses to review its collaborations and adopts the status quo position of Swissuniversities [38] [39] . It is interesting to compare this position with the reaction of Swissuniversities during Russia's invasion of Ukraine, when the organization published:

This scientific cooperation must not serve to support the aggressive policy of the Russian government, which with this war of aggression violates fundamental principles of human rights, international law and basic European values. In this sense, the Board of Swissuniversities recommends that Swiss universities review their scientific collaborations with universities in Russia and suspend them where such a danger exists. [40]

There is no mention of aggressive policy or human rights violations when Swissuniversities talks about Israel.

 

A sham ethical oversight

In June 2024, after two peaceful occupations and discussions with students, EPFL asked its advisory committee on the ethics of collaborations, GEP (Global Ethics and Partnership Committee), to produce an anonymous ethical report on collaborations with Israeli universities[41] . The report concluded that the collaborations posed no ethical concerns and that the Israeli universities involved did not explicitly support the army. However, it was publicly stated on the HUJI website that the university sends logistical material to the army[42] . The report makes no mention of the military barracks on the HUJI campus or the

 training programs for Israeli military officers provided by HUJI and TAU. To analyze the military scope of the collaborations, the GEP relied on abstracts, university websites, and... ChatGPT. Indeed, the committee, which does not even see military barracks on a campus, produces its ethical analyses with ChatGPT. In its conclusion, the report states: Israeli universities and major research institutions have frequent and deep relations with the Israeli defense industry and forces. However, no analysis of the ethical consequences of collaborations with such universities follows.
Nobody in the committee is actually competent in ethics

The very structure of the GEP is problematic. The majority of internal decision-makers have no declared ethical expertise, which does not comply with European standards of independence and competence required for ethics committees. Risk assessment is based on the university's reputational risk rather than on ethical risks per se. There is also a lack of transparency: its guidelines are not public, and only senior officials can refer matters to it, excluding the entire EPFL community.

 

 

 

 

Reporting students to the police

 

On May 22, 2025, around 50 people took part in a spontaneous peaceful demonstration at EPFL during the Vivapoly festival. The demonstration was against the presence of a Zionist student association, in the midst of the genocide in Gaza. After about 20 minutes, EPFL private security used force. First, they pinned a person to the ground under the Rolex, then forced the students to leave the festival. The gathering continued elsewhere, and security again pinned someone to the ground and strangled them. This person had to go to the emergency room afterwards. Following these events, instead of condemning its security service, the EPFL presidency continued its policy of censorship and repression by initiating disciplinary proceedings against a group of protesters. The school then filed a complaint with the public prosecutor, criminalizing the right to protest on campus, which is protected by international law. In the midst of genocide and after more than 680,000 deaths in two years[43] , under the bombs of the Zionist army, criminalizing the struggle for Palestine by attacking these students demonstrates our university's unconditional support for Israeli policy. It is more than urgent to end our bloody complicity with Zionist institutions.

●       Securing Modern Systems (cybersecurity and computer systems) — a field directly linked to Israeli intelligence and cyberwarfare units[46] .

These conferences and collaborations are therefore not neutral: they are part of a continuum in which academic research also serves to perfect the war machine while polishing a progressive image of Zionism. This intertwining of civilian research and military applications has been documented again recently:  The Iranian attack on the Weizmann Institute in June 2025 revealed the extent of European funding that flows into these militarized institutions — with a dozen EU-funded projects directly impacted, exposing how European research funds indirectly fuel weapons development centers[50] . While the Weizmann Institute is simultaneously signing agreements with the arms giant Elbit Systems, the international debate is intensifying: more and more universities around the world are severing their partnerships with Israeli universities, explicitly recognizing their complicity in Israel's military efforts—from training soldiers to developing military technologies in the region—and their participation in the genocide in Gaza
